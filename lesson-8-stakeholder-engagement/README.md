# Lesson 8: Stakeholder Engagement

## Overview

This lesson covers stakeholder engagement strategies for AI governance, including engagement planning, community impact assessment, advisory board design, and feedback mechanisms. Students learn to identify diverse stakeholder groups, assess community impacts, establish governance advisory structures, and design feedback loops — with particular focus on AI systems that serve vulnerable populations such as minors.

## Demo

**Scenario: LearnSpark AI** — An EdTech startup (Series B, 15 partner universities, 50,000+ students) building an AI-powered personalized STEM tutoring platform for college students. Compliance requirements include FERPA, institutional data sharing agreements, and ADA/Section 508 accessibility standards.

The instructor walks through the workbook live, filling in stakeholder engagement plans and feedback mechanisms for LearnSpark AI. The demo covers 3 sheets:

1. **Engagement Plan** — Define primary concerns, engagement methods, success metrics, and priority levels for 4 stakeholder groups (college STEM students, faculty & instructors, university administration, university IT departments).
2. **Feedback Mechanisms** — Design 3 collection mechanisms (student satisfaction surveys, faculty focus groups, technical integration reviews) with collection methods, response SLAs, escalation paths, and governance integration.

## Exercise

**Scenario: EduBridge AI** — A Series D EdTech company (200+ school districts, 500,000+ students, 25,000+ teachers) deploying an AI-powered adaptive learning platform for K-12 students across all subjects. Compliance requirements include FERPA, COPPA (children under 13), state student privacy laws, ADA/Section 508, and district data sharing agreements.

### Task

Students complete five governance deliverables across 5 sheets:

1. **Engagement Plan** — Evaluate 9 stakeholder groups including pre-filled groups (K-5 students, parents/guardians, teachers, school administrators, data privacy officers) and groups requiring student input (6-12 students, school board members, child safety advocates). For each group, define primary concerns, engagement methods, success metrics, and priority level.
2. **Community Impact Assessment** — Assess 6 impact areas (educational outcomes, digital divide, student privacy, algorithmic bias in learning paths, accessibility for special needs, cultural sensitivity and representation). Pre-filled rows provide structure; students complete likelihood/severity scores (1–5) and mitigation strategies for the last 3 rows.
3. **Advisory Board Charter** — Review 7 pre-filled charter elements (mission, member criteria, term length, meeting frequency, decision-making, reporting, conflict of interest) and complete 3 remaining elements: purpose, scope, and composition for the advisory board.
4. **Feedback Mechanisms** — Design 6 feedback mechanisms. 4 are pre-filled (K-5 surveys, parent portal, teacher focus groups, annual review); students complete 2 remaining mechanisms (incident reporting system and community town halls) with collection methods, response SLAs, escalation paths, and governance integration.
